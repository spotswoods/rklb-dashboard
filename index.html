        03 · GROWTH ENGINES

        Neutron & Space Systems — Rocket Lab's Two Paths to Scale

        Neutron is the 13-tonne-class, partially reusable medium-lift rocket targeting a Q4 2026 maiden flight — the leap from small launch into mega-constellation and national-security launch, and the single biggest catalyst (and risk) in the story. Space Systems is already the larger segment (66.9% of FY25 revenue), built into an end-to-end space company through years of acquisitions — Photon buses, the mass-producible Flatellite, and an in-house component stack (solar, reaction wheels, separation, EO/IR sensors via Geost, laser comms via Mynaric) that anchors $1.3B+ of SDA prime contracts.

            08 · BACKLOG & CONTRACTS

            $2.2B record backlog · +108% YoY · split Launch + Space Systems

            Backlog hit a record $2.2B at 3/31/26 (+108% YoY, +20% QoQ), anchored by $1.3B+ of SDA prime contracts and a launch manifest that grew by 31 new Electron/HASTE deals in Q1 alone. The open question is conversion to revenue and on-time delivery — booking a contract and recognizing it are different things, especially on multi-year defense programs. Backlog ≠ recognized revenue.

            Golden Dome & rising defense-space budgets — a structural tailwind, but appropriations-dependent
